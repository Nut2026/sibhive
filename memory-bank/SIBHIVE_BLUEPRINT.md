# Web App Blueprint: Sibhive

## A Complete Project Description for AI-Powered Sibling Connection

---

## 1. Project Overview

### 1.1 Vision Statement

**Sibhive** is a web application that creates a nurturing digital "hive" - a warm, sweet, and supportive space where siblings can grow together. Like a beehive, the app provides structure, safety, and sustenance for the sibling relationship. Every member has a role, every contribution is valued, and together they create something sweeter than they could alone. Using artificial intelligence, the app cultivates meaningful connections between siblings by generating collaborative activities, facilitating conflict resolution, and building a shared history of positive experiences.

### 1.2 Core Philosophy

The app is built on three fundamental beliefs:
1. **Sibling relationships are the longest relationships most people will have** - longer than parents, spouses, or children
2. **Technology can be a hive, not a barrier** - the same devices that disconnect siblings can become a warm, buzzing shared space when used intentionally
3. **Every sibling pair has untapped potential for connection** - the app helps them discover and nurture that potential, drop by drop

### 1.3 The Hive Metaphor

Just like a beehive:
- **Bees work together** - siblings collaborate on activities and build shared experiences
- **The atmosphere is warm and sweet** - the app fosters warmth, connection, and positivity
- **Sometimes it stings** - conflict happens, but the hive provides a structure to heal
- **The heart always outlasts the conflict** - the long-term relationship is always more important than any single disagreement
- **Every bee has a role** - each sibling contributes uniquely to the relationship
- **Nectar is collected** - experiences, memories, and positive interactions are gathered like precious nectar
- **The hive grows sweeter over time** - consistent effort builds a richer, more connected relationship

### 1.4 Target Audience

**Primary Users:**
- Children aged 8-16 with one or more siblings
- Families with 2-4 children in the household

**Secondary Users:**
- Parents seeking to reduce sibling conflict
- Adult siblings (18+) wanting to reconnect or strengthen bonds
- Blended families navigating new sibling dynamics

**Tertiary Users:**
- Counselors and therapists working with family dynamics
- Grandparents wanting to connect with multiple grandchildren simultaneously

## 2. Core Concept Architecture

### 2.1 The Hive Structure

The Sibhive app is organized like a beehive, with different "chambers" serving different purposes:

**Hive Heart (Home Screen):**
- "Your daily dose of connection"
- Where new activities and prompts are hatched
- Daily updates and fresh opportunities for connection
- Sibling's and the family's dashboard
- The heart of the hive where siblings start their day

**Honeycomb (Activity Dashboard):**
- Where siblings see their completed/due/upcoming activities
- Nectar points collected from each activity
- Visual representation of the hive's sweetness

**Pollen Garden (Memory Chamber):**
- Where memories are planted, grown, and harvested
- Shared digital artifacts that represent the relationship
- A growing collection of sweet moments

**Healing Chamber (Peace Corner):**
- Where stings are soothed and wounds are healed
- Guided conflict resolution
- A safe space to repair and reconnect

**Conversation Comb (Chat):**
- Where siblings record a conversation together
- There is an Echo Library that analyses the conversation
  - Recent topics
  - Inside jokes

### 2.2 The Three Hive Pillars

The app rests on three interconnected pillars that support healthy sibling relationships:

**Pillar 1: Honey Making (Shared Experiences)**
- Collaborative activities siblings complete together
- Building a shared history and inside jokes
- Creating digital artifacts (stories, drawings, playlists) that represent their unique bond
- Collecting Nectar (experience points) from each completed activity

**Pillar 2: Hive Warming (Emotional Safety)**
- Safe spaces to express feelings about the sibling relationship
- AI-facilitated perspective-taking exercises
- Learning each other's emotional languages
- Creating a warm, sweet atmosphere of trust

**Pillar 3: Healing (Conflict Resolution)**
- Tools to de-escalate arguments (soothing the sting)
- Guided mediation with AI-generated perspective stories
- Building repair skills for when conflicts occur
- Teaching that the heart always outlasts the conflict

### 2.3 User Types and Interfaces

The app has three distinct interfaces, each tailored to its user:

**Child Interface (Ages 8-12)**
- Warm, hive-like design with honeycomb patterns and soft colors
- Simple, icon-driven navigation
- Emphasis on visual and audio elements
- Limited text, more images and emojis
- Voice input option for younger children
- Bee mascot ("Buz") guides them through the hive

**Teen Interface (Ages 13-16)**
- Clean, modern design with warm, honey-toned touches
- More text-based interaction
- Privacy-focused features (can choose what to share)
- Integration with social media-like elements (timeline, stories)
- More sophisticated hive metaphors

**Parent/Guardian Interface**
- Dashboard with hive insights (sweetness metrics)
- Configuration and oversight tools
- Weekly summary reports (hive health updates)
- Content approval and moderation controls
- Educational resources about sibling dynamics

### Feature 1: Hive Activities (Collaborative Activities)

#### 1.1 Feature Overview

**Purpose:** To create shared experiences that build positive memories and teach collaboration skills, while collecting Nectar (experience points) that sweeten the hive.

**Description:** The AI generates weekly activities that siblings complete together. Each activity is designed to be fun, engaging, and developmentally appropriate while subtly teaching cooperation, communication, and empathy. Successful completion rewards siblings with Nectar.

#### 1.2 Activity Categories

**Category A: Honey Making (Collaborative Challenges)**
- Siblings work together to build, create, or solve something
- Examples: "Build a pillow fort together," "Create a family tree collage," "Design a board game"
- Teaches: Cooperation, division of labor, shared decision-making
- Nectar Reward: 50-75 drops

**Category B: Pollen Gathering (Story Weaving)**
- Siblings take turns creating a shared narrative
- Examples: "Each sibling records a 30-second part of a story about a magical forest," "Create a comic strip together about your day"
- Teaches: Active listening, building on others' ideas, creativity
- Nectar Reward: 40-60 drops

**Category C: Pollination (Teaching Moments)**
- One sibling teaches another something they're good at
- Examples: "Older sibling teaches younger a video game trick," "Younger sibling teaches older a dance they learned"
- Teaches: Patience, leadership, appreciation of each other's skills
- Nectar Reward: 45-65 drops

**Category D: Hive Heart (Emotional Check-ins)**
- Siblings share feelings and learn about each other's inner worlds
- Examples: "Share one thing that made you happy this week and one thing that made you sad," "Draw how you're feeling and explain it to each other"
- Teaches: Emotional vocabulary, vulnerability, empathy
- Nectar Reward: 55-80 drops

**Category E: Honeycomb Treasures (Memory Makers)**
- Creating something permanent that represents their bond
- Examples: "Create a sibling playlist," "Make a time capsule," "Film a sibling interview"
- Teaches: Shared identity, appreciation, legacy
- Nectar Reward: 60-85 drops

#### 1.3 Activity Generation Flow

1. **Input Collection:**
   - Each sibling provides interests, hobbies, and favorite activities
   - Parent sets preferred activity difficulty level
   - AI tracks past activities to avoid repetition

2. **AI Generation:**
   - GPT-5.6 receives: sibling ages, interests, past activity history, current relationship dynamics (from parent input), and desired learning outcomes
   - Generates 3-5 activity options
   - Filters for age-appropriateness and safety

3. **Parent Review (Optional):**
   - Parent can approve, modify, or reject generated activities
   - Parent can set activity schedule (daily, weekly, or manual)

4. **Activity Presentation:**
   - Activity appears on each sibling's home screen with Buz the bee announcing it
   - Clear instructions with visual aids
   - Estimated completion time displayed
   - Required materials list (if any)
   - Potential Nectar reward displayed

5. **Activity Completion:**
   - Siblings submit proof of completion (photo, text, audio, video)
   - AI analyzes submission to confirm completion
   - Both siblings receive Nectar and badges ("honey jars")
   - Celebration animation (bees dancing, honey dripping)

#### 1.4 Activity Progression System

**Levels of Difficulty:**
- **Level 1 (Easy):** Activities taking 5-10 minutes, minimal supervision needed - Nectar: 30-40 drops
- **Level 2 (Medium):** Activities taking 10-20 minutes, some parental guidance recommended - Nectar: 45-60 drops
- **Level 3 (Hard):** Activities taking 20-30 minutes, parent involvement encouraged - Nectar: 65-85 drops

**Activity Streaks:**
- Completing activities on consecutive weeks unlocks bonus Nectar
- Streak milestones: 4 weeks, 8 weeks, 12 weeks, 24 weeks, 52 weeks
- Each milestone awards bonus Nectar and special honey jar badges

**Seasonal Activities:**
- Special activities for holidays, birthdays, school breaks
- Example: "Summer Nectar Hunt," "Holiday Honey Collection"

### Feature 3: The Healing Chamber (Conflict Resolution)

#### 3.1 Feature Overview

**Purpose:** To help siblings resolve conflicts constructively and learn perspective-taking skills, teaching that the heart always outlasts the conflict.

**Description:** When siblings argue, the "Healing Chamber" feature provides guided mediation using AI-generated perspective stories and discussion prompts. It soothes the sting and helps the hive heal.

#### 3.2 Conflict Resolution Flow

**Step 1: Enter the Healing Chamber**
- Parent activates the Healing Chamber feature after a conflict
- Parent provides brief description (age-appropriate, neutral tone)
- Parent selects which siblings are involved
- A soothing, calm interface appears (soft colors, gentle animations)

**Step 2: Individual Pollen Gatherings**
- Each sibling is interviewed separately (in private, using text or voice)
- AI asks: "What happened from your point of view?" "How did you feel?" "What were you hoping would happen?"
- Siblings can answer freely without interruption
- Responses are not shared with other sibling
- Like bees gathering pollen from different flowers

**Step 3: AI Honey Making (Perspective Generation)**
- GPT-5.6 analyzes both siblings' accounts
- Generates a neutral, empathetic "Perspective Story" for each sibling
- Story presents the other sibling's viewpoint in a relatable way
- Language: Age-appropriate, non-judgmental, focuses on feelings
- Stories are presented as "honey drops of understanding"

**Step 4: Guided Healing Discussion**
- Parent and both siblings sit together
- Each sibling reads the other's Perspective Story (or has it read to them)
- AI provides discussion prompts: "How do you think your sibling felt?" "What could you have done differently?"
- Parent guides the discussion using AI suggestions
- The focus is on healing, not blame

**Step 5: Repair and Reconciliation**
- Siblings are prompted to offer a genuine apology or repair
- AI generates a "Honey Promise" for each sibling: "I promise to..."
- Siblings "seal" their Honey Promise with a digital signature
- Conflict is logged and tracked for patterns
- A small amount of Nectar is awarded for healing (10-20 drops)

**Step 6: Follow-Up**
- AI suggests a "Repair Activity" to rebuild trust
- Example: "Do something kind for your sibling today and share what you did"
- Parent receives summary of resolution and suggested next steps
- A "Healing Badge" is added to the hive

#### 3.3 Conflict Types and AI Responses

**Type A: Resource Conflict (Toy, device, attention)**
- AI focuses on: Sharing, turn-taking, empathy
- Perspective story highlights the other's desire for the resource
- Honey Promise examples: "I promise to share," "I promise to take turns"

**Type B: Accusation Conflict ("You always...")**
- AI focuses on: Specificity, fairness, avoiding generalizations
- Perspective story highlights the other's feelings about being blamed
- Honey Promise examples: "I promise to be more specific," "I promise to listen"

**Type C: Hurt Feelings Conflict (Exclusion, teasing)**
- AI focuses on: Emotional impact, apology, repair
- Perspective story highlights the hurt child's experience
- Honey Promise examples: "I promise to be kind," "I promise to include you"

**Type D: Sibling Rivalry Conflict (Competition)**
- AI focuses on: Validation, individual strengths, cooperation
- Perspective story highlights each child's need to be seen
- Honey Promise examples: "I promise to celebrate your wins," "I promise to be happy for you"

#### 3.4 Parent Mediation Support

**Mediation Scripts:**
- AI generates parent-specific scripts for the discussion
- Scripts include: Opening statements, prompts for each child, closing remarks
- Adapts to different conflict types
- Uses gentle, healing language

**Calm Down Tools:**
- Quick breathing exercises for both siblings (visualized as bees calming down)
- Timer for taking space before mediation
- Soothing sound options (gentle buzzing, soft honey drip sounds)

**When to Seek Help:**
- AI flags concerning patterns (escalating frequency or severity)
- Provides suggestions for professional help
- Resources for finding family therapists

### Feature 4: Daily Nectar Prompts

#### 4.1 Feature Overview

**Purpose:** To encourage regular, meaningful communication between siblings beyond just "how was your day?"

**Description:** Each day, the AI generates conversation prompts for siblings. Prompts are delivered individually to each sibling, and they can choose to share their responses with their sibling(s). Responding earns Nectar.

#### 4.2 Prompt Categories

**Category A: Fun & Light Prompts**
- "If you could swap lives with anyone for a day, who would it be?"
- "What's the funniest thing that happened this week?"
- "If you could have any superpower, what would it be?"
- Nectar Reward: 10-15 drops

**Category B: Deep Prompts**
- "What's something you wish your sibling understood about you?"
- "What's your favorite memory with your sibling?"
- "What does 'being a good sibling' mean to you?"
- Nectar Reward: 15-20 drops

**Category C: Gratitude Prompts**
- "What's something your sibling did that made you feel good?"
- "What's one thing you appreciate about your sibling?"
- "What's something you're grateful for today?"
- Nectar Reward: 15-20 drops

**Category D: Sibling-Specific Prompts**
- "What's a funny inside joke you have with your sibling?"
- "What's something you and your sibling disagree about?"
- "What's something you'd like to do together?"
- Nectar Reward: 12-18 drops

**Category E: Future Prompts**
- "What do you think your relationship with your sibling will be like in 10 years?"
- "What's one thing you'd like to change about your relationship?"
- "What's a goal you'd like to achieve with your sibling?"
- Nectar Reward: 15-20 drops

#### 4.3 Prompt Delivery

**Daily Schedule:**
- Prompt delivered in the morning (optional: parent sets time)
- Siblings receive prompt privately on their devices
- Siblings decide if and when to respond
- Buz the bee announces the prompt

**Response Options:**
- Text response
- Voice recording
- Photo or image
- Video message
- Each response earns Nectar

**Privacy Settings:**
- Siblings choose if responses are shared immediately or later
- Parent has visibility into whether prompts are answered (not the content, unless siblings agree)
- Teen can choose to keep some responses private

#### 4.4 Conversation History

**Honeycomb Storage:**
- All shared responses are saved in a conversation history
- Siblings can browse past conversations
- AI can surface memories: "Remember when you said..."
- Responses add to the hive's sweetness

**Family Dinner Mode:**
- AI compiles recent prompts for family dinner discussion
- Parent can use as conversation starters

### Feature 5: Pollen Garden (Memory Nest)

#### 5.1 Feature Overview

**Purpose:** To create a shared, living history of the sibling relationship.

**Description:** A collaborative digital garden that grows over time with photos, stories, artifacts, and memories created by siblings together. Each memory is like pollen collected from their shared experiences.

#### 5.2 Pollen Garden Components

**Core Artifacts:**
- **Written Memories:** Shared stories, journal entries, letters
- **Visual Memory:** Photos, drawings, digital art
- **Audio Memory:** Voice recordings, interviews, songs
- **Video Memory:** Short videos, time-lapse projects
- **Digital Artifacts:** Collaborative playlists, recipe collections

**Memory Categories:**
- **Milestones:** Firsts, achievements, growth moments
- **Everyday Joys:** Simple happy moments, routines
- **Adventures:** Trips, outings, special events
- **Learning Moments:** Things they taught each other
- **Inside Jokes:** Silly moments, memes, shared humor

#### 5.3 Artifact Creation Flow

1. **Initiation:**
   - A sibling starts creating a memory artifact
   - Selects category and type
   - Adds title and description
   - Buz the bee offers encouragement

2. **Collaboration:**
   - Other siblings are invited to contribute
   - AI suggests additions: "Try adding a photo from that day"
   - Co-editing tools for stories and art

3. **AI Enhancement:**
   - GPT-5.6 generates "Memory Bloomers" - questions to make the memory more vivid
   - "What did the weather feel like?" "What did you eat that day?"
   - "What was your favorite part?"

4. **Finalization:**
   - All siblings approve final version
   - Artifact is added to shared Pollen Garden
   - A gentle celebration animation appears (flowers blooming, bees dancing)
   - Nectar is awarded (20-30 drops)

5. **Memory Bloom:**
   - Periodically, AI surfaces old memories
   - "One year ago today, you created this memory together!"
   - Prompts: "What would you add to this memory now?"

#### 5.4 Memory Prompts

**Daily Memory Prompt:**

**Weekly Memory Prompt:**
- "What's the best thing you and your sibling did together this week?"

**Random Memory Prompt:**

### Feature 6: Conversation Comb (Sibling Audio Archive)

#### 6.1 Feature Overview

**Purpose:** To create a living audio archive of sibling connection—capturing voices, laughter, inside jokes, and meaningful conversations that build a lasting record of their relationship.

**Description:** A dedicated space where siblings record and store their actual conversations together. Every laugh, inside joke, and meaningful moment is preserved like honey in a comb. Siblings can initiate recordings, listen back to past conversations, and build a rich audio history of their relationship. It's not about typing—it's about being together.

#### 6.2 Core Features

**Recording Features:**
- Two-person simultaneous recording (both siblings record together)
- Voice-only capture (no video required)
- Unlimited duration with pause/resume capability
- Auto-save on exit

**Organization Features:**
- Conversation library with chronological view
- Smart AI categorization (laughter, deep talk, planning, healing, etc.)
- Custom tags and labels
- Search and filter by date, tag, duration, or mood
- Favorites and starring system

**Playback Features:**
- Speed control (0.5x - 2.0x)
- AI-identified key moments (laughter, emotional highlights)
- Highlight creation (save 30-second clips)
- Optional AI-generated text transcripts
- Background playback

#### 6.3 Special Features

- **Inside Joke Vault:** Siblings can "vault" specific moments from conversations, creating a dedicated collection of shared humor. Can be "planted" in the Pollen Garden as memory artifacts. *(10 nectar drops)*

- **Conversation Starters:** AI suggests prompts based on time of day, recent activities, and relationship dynamics. Examples: "Tell each other about the best part of your day," "Share one thing you're worried about." *(15 nectar drops)*

- **Goodnight Echoes:** Nightly recording ritual where siblings share one sweet thing about their day. Creates a beautiful archive of daily check-ins. *(10 nectar drops)* + 50 bonus drops for 7-day streak

- **Echo Collections:** Siblings can create themed collections of conversations (e.g., "Summer 2026," "Deep Talks," "Funniest Moments"). Collections can be shared with parents or kept private. *(20 nectar drops)*

- **Echo Tags:** Custom tagging system with pre-set options (😂 Laughter, 💬 Deep Talk, 🎵 Musical, 🤝 Planning, 🔥 Heated, 🎯 Inside Joke, 💛 Gratitude) plus ability to create custom tags. AI auto-suggests tags based on analysis. *(5 nectar drops per tag)*

- **Conversation Calendar:** Visual calendar showing recording patterns, heat map of frequency, streak tracking, and gentle reminders for empty days. *(5 nectar drops per conversation day)*

#### 6.4 AI Support in The Conversation Comb

- **Smart Prompt Generation:** AI creates tailored conversation prompts based on sibling ages, past conversations, time of day, recent activities, and relationship dynamics.

- **Conversation Analysis:** AI analyzes recordings for emotional tone (joy, sadness, frustration, excitement), laughter frequency, topic identification, turn-taking patterns, and engagement levels. Provides insights like "You laughed 12 times in this conversation!" or "Great listening - you took turns evenly!"

- **Conflict Detection:** Real-time detection of raised voices, frustration, interrupting patterns, or negative language. Offers gentle intervention: "It sounds like things are getting heated. Would you like to pause and try the Healing Chamber?" Auto-routes to Healing Chamber if conflict persists.

- **Emotional Support:** Detects sadness, loneliness, or anxiety in voice and offers support options: "Your sibling sounds sad. Would you like to send them a virtual hug? 🤗" Notifies parents if concerning patterns emerge.

- **Echo Insights:** Provides metrics on total recording time, most active days, emotional trends, growth in conversation depth, and favorite topics. Visualized through charts, word clouds, and emotion trend graphs.

- **Echo Highlights:** AI creates highlight reels of best moments, "Remember when..." suggestions, anniversary reminders, and "This day last year" compilations. *(30 nectar drops)*

#### 6.5 Safety Features

**Privacy & Consent:**
- Two-person consent required (both siblings must agree to record)
- Clear visual indicator when recording is active
- Either sibling can stop recording at any time
- Auto-stop after 5 minutes of silence
- Both siblings must confirm deletion of any recording

**Content Safety:**
- Voice-only focus (no video, reduced privacy concerns)
- AI filtering for inappropriate content (bullying, harassment)
- Sibling flagging system for concerning conversations
- Flagged content reviewed by parent and/or app moderation team

**Parental Controls:**
- Parents see recording frequency, duration, and tags (not content, unless flagged)
- Opt-in required for children to use this feature
- Daily recording time limits
- Parent can request full archive access (requires both siblings' consent)
- Parent can request deletion of specific recordings

**Age-Appropriate Features:**
- **8-12 Years:** Pre-set prompts, playful interface, Buz the bee guidance, shorter recommended duration (2-8 minutes), simple tags
- **13-16 Years:** Full feature access, privacy options, advanced tags and collections, longer recommended duration (5-15 minutes), custom prompt creation

### 6.4 Conflict Flow (Parent + Children)

**Trigger:**
- Parent notices conflict (hears argument, children report)
- Parent opens app, taps "Healing Chamber" icon
- Parent selects conflict type and provides brief description
- A soothing, calm interface appears

**Step 1: Separate Pollen Gatherings:**
- Child 1 goes to a private space with device
- Child 1 answers questions (typed or spoken)
  - "What happened from your point of view?"
  - "How did you feel during the argument?"
  - "What were you hoping would happen instead?"
- Child 1 answers all questions
- Child 2 repeats the process in a separate space
- Each child earns a small amount of Nectar for participating (5 drops)

**Step 2: AI Honey Making:**
- GPT-5.6 analyzes both responses
- Generates a "Perspective Story" (honey drop of understanding) for each child
- Creates mediation script for parent
- Prepares discussion prompts
- A "Healing Badge" is prepared for resolution

**Step 3: Guided Healing (Parent-led):**
- Parent gathers both siblings in a calm space
- Parent reads or displays Child 1's Perspective Story
  - "This is what Child 1 experienced. Let's hear their story..."
- Parent reads or displays Child 2's Perspective Story
  - "This is what Child 2 experienced. Let's hear their story..."
- Parent uses AI-generated prompts to facilitate discussion:
  - "How did you feel when you heard Child 1's story?"
  - "What could you have done differently?"
  - "What can we do to make things better?"

**Step 4: Reconciliation and Repair:**
- Both siblings offer apologies or repair statements
- Each child writes a "Honey Promise" (with AI suggestions)
  - Example: "I promise to ask before borrowing your things."
  - Example: "I promise to share my toys when you ask nicely."
- Both siblings "seal" their Honey Promise (digital signatures)
- System creates a "Healing Artifact" (digital certificate)
- Nectar awarded for healing (15-20 drops each)
- "Healing Badge" awarded to both siblings

**Step 5: Follow-Up:**
- AI suggests a "Repair Activity" for the next day
  - Example: "Do something kind for your sibling today and share what you did."
- Parent receives summary of resolution
- Conflict is logged for pattern tracking
- A gentle reminder: "Remember, the heart always outlasts the conflict"

## 7. AI Integration Details

### 7.1 GPT-5.6 Usage Cases

**Case 1: Activity Generation (Sol Model)**
- **Input:** Sibling ages, interests, past activity history, current relationship dynamics, desired learning outcomes
- **Output:** 3-5 activity options with title, description, instructions, materials, estimated duration, nectar reward
- **Parameters:**
  - temperature: 0.7 (balanced creativity)
  - max_tokens: 500
  - safety_filters: age-appropriate content
- **Prompt Template:** "You are a warm, nurturing AI that creates activities for siblings. The activities should be fun, collaborative, and developmentally appropriate. Use the hive metaphor naturally."

**Case 2: Perspective Story Generation (Sol Model)**
- **Input:** Each sibling's conflict interview responses, relationship type, age
- **Output:** Empathetic, age-appropriate narrative presenting the other's viewpoint (a "honey drop of understanding")
- **Parameters:**
  - temperature: 0.5 (truthful, empathetic)
  - max_tokens: 300
  - tone: warm, non-judgmental, focused on feelings
- **Prompt Template:** "You are a wise, gentle bee helping siblings understand each other. Create a story that presents one sibling's perspective to the other in a kind, relatable way."

**Case 3: Daily Prompt Generation (Terra Model)**
- **Input:** Sibling ages, previous responses, time of year, current events
- **Output:** 1-2 conversation prompts with categories and nectar rewards
- **Parameters:**
  - temperature: 0.8 (creative)
  - max_tokens: 150
- **Prompt Template:** "You are a friendly bee named Buz, helping siblings connect. Create a daily question that encourages sharing and understanding."

**Case 4: Hive Sweetness Score Calculation (Luna Model)**
- **Input:** Activity completion data, prompt engagement, conflict patterns, mood data, nectar earned
- **Output:** Numeric score (0-100) and textual insight
- **Parameters:**
  - temperature: 0.3 (consistent, analytical)
  - max_tokens: 100
- **Formula:** (Activity Completion Rate × 40%) + (Prompt Engagement × 25%) + (Positive Interactions × 20%) + (Conflict Resolution × 15%)

**Case 5: Insight Generation (Sol Model)**
- **Input:** Family relationship data, trends, patterns, nectar collection history
- **Output:** 2-3 actionable insights for parents
- **Parameters:**
  - temperature: 0.4 (helpful, specific)
  - max_tokens: 200
- **Prompt Template:** "You are a wise elder bee helping a parent understand their children's sibling dynamics. Provide warm, practical, evidence-based insights."

**Case 6: Memory Bloomer (Terra Model)**
- **Input:** Memory artifact title, description, siblings' ages
- **Output:** 3-5 questions to make the memory more vivid
- **Parameters:**
  - temperature: 0.8 (creative, evocative)
  - max_tokens: 200
- **Prompt Template:** "You are helping siblings make their memories richer. Ask questions that bring a memory to life - about feelings, details, and senses."

## 8. Security and Privacy

**Access Control:**
- Role-based access control:

  - Child: Own data, shared artifacts, sibling responses (if shared)
  - Sibling: Shared data only

**Data Minimization:**
- Only collect data necessary for features

- No sale of data to third parties

**Parental Consent:**
- Verifiable parental consent before account creation
- Full access to child's data and account
- Option to delete all data at any time

**Children Under 13:**
- No collection of personal information without consent
- No sharing of data without consent
- Age-appropriate content and ads (no ads)
- No chat with strangers (only verified family members)

**Automated Filtering:**
- Filter for profanity in messages and prompts
